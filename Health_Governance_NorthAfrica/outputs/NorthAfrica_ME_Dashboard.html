Model selection criteria: (1) Epidemiological plausibility — projections must not yield negative or biologically impossible values; (2) Historical fit quality (R²); (3) Consistency of direction beyond the training range; (4) Country-specific context (conflict, COVID spikes, governance stability). The logarithmic model is generally preferred for countries approaching low mortality levels (diminishing returns). Linear is preferred when the trend is steady and the floor is not yet approached. Polynomial (deg=2) should be used with caution — it often curves upward beyond the training range.
